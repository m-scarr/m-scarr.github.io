About Me

I am a Full Stack Developer living in Minneapolis.

Growing up, my love of videogames compelled me to create my own. This love drove me to study and master an object-oriented language used for designing games (GML) by the age of 16. Since then, and throughout my journey, I have developed a passion for software design and the creative problem-solving it requires. I picked up HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. My objective is to elevate my self-taught hobby into a full-time professional career.

In May of 2018, I earned a certificate from the University of Minnesota's Coding Bootcamp. I excelled with ease and found a new appreciation for web development.

My days are filled with learning new frameworks and implementations on the front and back end. I look forward to working with a professional development team!

I'm the complete package, curious about my hardware experience?
I also started fiddling with, breaking, and fixing computers at a young age. I've always been extremely competent at repairing OS problems, but was curious about hardware repair as well. When I turned 23, I got a job at a local video game store as a repair technician. I was trained by an older technician and learned a variety of high-level repairs. I learned how to micro-solder, as well as operate a processor reflow machine. I also learned how to replace phone screens and many other unique and difficult repairs. After about 3 years of working there, eventually as lead technician, I took a job working retail as the store could not afford to pay me a living wage.
